Five Things That Move Your Price

  • How long you keep the unit — month-long site rentals carry the lowest cost per day.
  • How often it gets pumped — once a week is included; busy units needing extra visits cost more.
  • Which unit you pick — basic units cost least, trailers the most.
  • How far we truck it from our Atlanta yard.
  • When you book — spring festival season fills routes fast, so early bookings price better.

Pricing Questions, Answered

What's the cheapest porta potty option in Atlanta?

Long-term construction rentals at $175 to $400 per month deliver the lowest per-day price. For one-off events, a basic unit booked a couple of weeks ahead keeps you at the low end of the $175 to $280 weekly band.

Are delivery and cleaning extra?

No — every quote bundles drop-off, placement, weekly pumping and restocking, and final collection. The usual extras are additional service visits and long-haul delivery.

Why is there a range instead of one price?

Service frequency is the main swing factor. One clean a week sits at the bottom of the band; two or three pushes the price up. Distance and season do the rest.

What do accessible units cost in Atlanta?

ADA-compliant units run $250 – $360 weekly here. If your event is open to the public, plan at least one accessible unit per bank of standard ones.

What does a restroom trailer go for?

Two-stall trailers with flushing toilets, sinks and climate control start around $450 – $900 for a day or weekend in the Atlanta area; bigger trailers are quoted per event.
